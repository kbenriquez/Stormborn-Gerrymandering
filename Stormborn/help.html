What is Gerrymandering?


In the process of setting electoral districts, gerrymandering is a practice intended to establish a political advantage for a particular party or group by manipulating district boundaries. The resulting district is known as a gerrymander (/ˈdʒɛriˌmændər/, recently changed from the original /ˈɡɛriˌmændər/); however, that word can also refer to the process. The term gerrymandering has negative connotations. Two principal tactics are used in gerrymandering: "cracking" (i.e. diluting the voting power of the opposing party's supporters across many districts) and "packing" (concentrating the opposing party's voting power in one district to reduce their voting power in other districts). In addition to its use achieving desired electoral results for a particular party, gerrymandering may be used to help or hinder a particular demographic, such as a political, ethnic, racial, linguistic, religious, or class group, such as in U.S. federal voting district boundaries that produce a majority of constituents representative of African-American or other racial minorities, known as "majority-minority districts". Gerrymandering can also be used to protect incumbents.

About The App


Welcome to the Stormborn Gerrymandering/Reverse Gerrymandering application. In this app, you can view district election results for the past elections and see a detailed breakdown of district and state information such as demographics and total votes. Learn how to use the app below!











The Home Page

HOME



Overview




On the Home Page, you can view a state's information when you hover over a state. You will see on the upper-right corner of the map the Republican and Democratic votes cast in that state. You can also see demographic information of a particular state when you select a state and a year below the map. Demographic information will be shown as charts below the map. To see district information, click on a state to zoom in or click on "District Zoom" and the map will zoom in to show the districts.

Running Tests




Right under the map, you will see in order from left to right: a dropdown list of the states of America, a dropdown list of years ranging from 1990 to 2016 in indeces of 2, 4 checkboxes of tests (more details below), a 'RUN TEST' button and a 'CLEAR RESULTS' button.

To run a test(s) on a state, first select a state on the map or from the dropdown, select from which year you want the data to be from and then choose up to 4 tests to execute. Click on 'RUN TEST' and the results from the test(s) will be shown in table format below.

The 4 tests: Efficiency Gap, Excess Seats, Lopsided Wins and Reliable Wins are tests for measuring gerrymandering. The Efficiency Gap is the ratio between casted wasted votes and casted total votes on a district.













The Super Districts Page

SUPER DISTRICTS



On the Super Districts Page, in addition to the features in the Home page (with the exception of the year dropdown), you can also click on multiple districts to create a super district (for the most recent congressional district). The rules for creating super districts are:

  • Districts must be adjacent
  • Districts must be within the same state
  • Depending on the state, a minimum of 2 or 3 districts must be selected to create a super district
  • Depending on the state, a maximum of 5 districts can be selected to create a super district
